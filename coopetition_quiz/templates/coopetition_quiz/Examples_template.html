Example 1: If both of you invest all 10 points in the Investment Account (\(x_1=x_2=10\)), this will generate a total return of 10×10=100 points. Because both of you invest 0 point in the Rationing Account (\(y_1=y_2=0\)), the profit is evenly split between you two (each of you has 0.5 of the total return). Therefore, each of you receives 50 points. This can be illustrated by the following table.

Investment AccountRationing AccountKept to self Total ReturnShare of Total ReturnFinal Earnings
You\(x_1=10\)\(y_1=0\)\(0\) \(x_1x_2=100\) \(0.5\)\(0.5\times 100 + 0 = 50\)
Other\(x_2=10\)\(y_2=0\)\(0\) \(0.5 \)\(0.5 \times 100 +0 = 50\)


Example 2: If both of you keep all 10 points to yourselves, you each receive these 10 points as your earnings because the total return from the investment is 0. This can be illustrated by the following table.

Investment AccountRationing AccountKept to self Total ReturnShare of Total ReturnFinal Earnings

Other\(x_2=0\)\(y_2=0\)\(10\) \(0.5\)\(0.5 \times 0 +10 = 10\)
