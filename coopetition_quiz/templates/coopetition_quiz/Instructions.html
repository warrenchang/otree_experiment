In this part, there will be 10 rounds of decision-making. You will be matched with another participant. You will play with the same participant for all 10 rounds.

Your decision

In each round, you receive 10 points. You need to decide whether to invest these points to an Investment Account and a Rationing Account, or keep these points to yourself.

Your earnings

Assume you and the other participant have the following allocation of the points:

Investment AccountRationing AccountKept to oneself
You\(x_1\)\(y_1\)\(10-x_1-y_1\)
Other\(x_2\)\(y_2\)\(10-x_2-y_2\)

That is, you put \(x_1\) points in the Investment Account, \(y_1\) points in the Rationing Account, and keep the rest \(10-x_1-y_1\) points; the other participant put \(x_2\) points in the Investment Account, \(y_2\) points in the Rationing Account, and keep the rest \(10-x_2-y_2\) points. Your earnings is calculated as follows:

{% if player.treatment == "Det0_60" %}
  • Points you kept for yourself are counted directly as part of your earnings.
  • Points in the Investment Account can generate appreciable return, which is shared between you and the other participant you are paired with. The size of this total return is: $$ \text{Total return} = x_1 x_2 $$
  • Your share of the total return is determined by the points you and the other participant put into the Rationing Account: $$ \text{Your share of the total return} = \frac{y_1}{y_1 + y_2} $$ In case both of you put 0 points in the Rationing Account, the total return will be evenly split between you two.
  • Your final earnings can be expressed as $$\text{Final Earnings} = \underbrace{\frac{y_1}{y_1 + y_2}x_1 x_2}_{\text{earnings from the investment}} + \underbrace{10-x_1-y_1}_{\text{points kept for oneself}}$$

In general, putting more points in the Investment Account (a higher \(x_1\)) generates a higher total return , while putting more points in the Rationing Account (a higher \(y_1\)) increases your share of the total return.
